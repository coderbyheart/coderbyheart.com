  • What is your take on the state of enterprise software from a user perspective? I think the "appification" is full on.
  • Where maybe 10 years back, users would invest time to "learn" a new user interface and functionality because software was seen as something
  • well worth investing in, because it was there to improve your day-to-day work, and you would accept some of its quirks and awkward UI.
  • But today, users in general are no longer willing to invest time into learning a new program and its UI features.
  • They expect simple screens that are self-explanatory and serve one simple goal.
  • Which is great, because I can now develop the features of a "suite" as independent products, with their own release cycles.
  • They share a SSO and the same UI paradigmas, but that is easy. As long as you give your users a "home screen" as a starting point,
  • they will be happily switching between the apps.
  • This enables you to have the ultimate usability: per use-case, context-sensitive UI instead of compromising for integrating everything.
